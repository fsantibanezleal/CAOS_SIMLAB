# 03 · S03 — SIR Epidemic (agent-based, on a grid)

**Problem statement.** A disease spreads by *local contact* through a population laid out on a square
lattice: each cell is one individual in health state **S**usceptible, **I**nfected or **R**ecovered, and
infection passes only between neighbouring cells. From per-cell rules — a susceptible cell catches the
disease from its infected Moore-neighbours with probability `β` each, an infected cell recovers with
probability `γ` per step and is then immune forever — we want the emergent epidemic curve: *whether* the
outbreak takes off or fizzles, how tall and when the **infected peak** is, and what fraction of the
population is ultimately infected (the **attack rate**). This is the **Kermack–McKendrick (1927)** SIR
model in its **agent-based (ABM)** form — a probabilistic cellular automaton, the discrete spatial analogue
of the classic compartmental SIR. It is built on **real Mesa 3** (`mesa.Agent` / `mesa.Model` /
`mesa.space.SingleGrid` / the model `AgentSet`) and runs seeded, so the run is a pure function of
`(params, seed)`.
