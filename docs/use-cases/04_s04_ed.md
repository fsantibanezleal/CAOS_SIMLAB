# 04 · S04 — Emergency Department Patient Flow

Patients arrive at an emergency department **non-stationarily** (with an optional daytime *surge*), pass
through **triage** (a pool of nurses, FCFS), then **treatment** (a pool of bays with *priority* — urgent
patients jump ahead of standard ones), and finally incur a fixed **discharge** delay before leaving.
Treatment is the bottleneck. The operational question is how the load, the bay staffing, the surge and the
urgent mix drive the mean **length-of-stay (LOS)** per class. The model is a **tandem priority queueing
network** with no closed form, run as a process-interaction DES on [SimPy](../frameworks/01_simpy.md),
seeded so its flow trace replays exactly. Because priority and non-stationary arrivals push it out of any
analytic regime, the honest measure is replicated simulation — not a single run.
